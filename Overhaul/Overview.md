Combat-focused ttrpg utilising a deck of cards instead of dice.

Turn-based system where everybody has a turn in a round based on which card is drawn. 

Deck – each player gets a small personal deck with which to draw or cast spells from. If your deck is used up, you must draw from the draw pile instead (you can still draw from the draw pile even if your deck is not used up)

Downtime – can last as much time as game master determines. Downtime shuffles the card pile, restores everyone's deck, and restores health and other resources.

Progression and class system – you start with 2 archetypes of your choice (or 1 specialised archetype of your choice), depending on your race and background. When you level up (happens at important points in the story), you may upgrade an existing archetype or gain a new one of a category you don't yet have. There are 3 categories – Heroic, Magical, and Buccaneer. Heroic focuses on direct combat ability, Magical on spells and utility, and Buccaneer on support and teamwork. Each archetype has one specialisation, which can be acquired by upgrading the archetype once.

Turns in combat – on your turn you have a movement action, a versatile action, and two archetype actions. Movement action allows you to move, archetype action allows you to use an archetype ability, and versatile action can be used for generic actions as well as archetype abilities.

Resources – universal: Health and Willpower. Health represents your ability to tolerate combat and willpower represents your will to keep fighting. If your health drops to 0, your willpower is halved and you take a wound (health increases by amount depending on archetype). If your willpower drops to 0, you are defeated and cannot keep fighting. Some archetypes will have abilities that interact with these systems.

### Game Setup
Centre:
- A draw deck
- A discard deck
The draw deck and discard deck are used for the primary card-based randomness of the game. When a card is used for something, it goes to the discard deck (which is face up)
When the draw deck is used up, flip the discard deck and use it as the new draw deck.

Players:
- A character sheet
- A hand
Each player should have a character sheet that describes the character and allows tracking of abilities and resources, as well as a hand of cards. When a card is used from a hand, it goes to the discard pile.

Other objects such as environments, maps, character minis, a DM screen, etc. are not necessary to play the game but can be used to enhanythe experience.

### How Sessions Work
Sessions can be run pretty freely, but the intended design is to have:
- Start with downtime
- Build up plot with in-combat and out of combat scenes
- Shorter downtime before climax
- Climactic battle
This system benefits more from planned pacing (like a serialised story) rather than a sandbox, but it can still be played that way.

### Measurements
Measurements will be done using 'units'. A unit can represent a variable space depending on who is playing and the situation at hand, but generally an average sized character takes up one 'unit' of space and can reach one 'unit' in every direction around him.
