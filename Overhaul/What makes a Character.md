A player character is made out of a combination of various different things: 
- Race
- Background
- Archetypes
- Formative Events
- Skills

Race and Background determine the archetype options and statistics of a character. They can also provide skills. Race primarily provides racial abilities and one archetype choice (usually between 2 archetype categories, but can be 3 for some races), and background provides skills and one archetype category choice (or upgrade if the character already has an archetype of that category).

Archetypes are the primary progression pathway and the source of most direct combat abilities. 

Formative events are separate from background and provide roleplaying pathways as well as skills. The recommended number of formative events for a character is 1-2 – remember, skills can be learned in game, provided sufficient roleplaying reason.
